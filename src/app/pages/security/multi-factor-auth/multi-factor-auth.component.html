Multi-Factor Authentication

We’ve taken additional steps to ensure that every user’s account is secure and accessible to them only. This is made possible through our two-factor authentication infrastructure which works with multiple authentication providers.

What is two-factor authentication?

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is a way of establishing access to an online account or computer system that requires the user to provide two different types of information. It provides the system you’re trying to access with additional evidence that you are the rightful owner of the account.

Enabling 2FA on your Reloadly account.

On your Reloadly dashboard, navigate to the Security section on the sidebar:

Similar to the image below, you will see a section with details of how 2FA works and the security applications supported by Reloadly’s infrastructure ( Google Authentication, Authy, Auth0 Guardian, and DUO ). Click on the Enable button, this enables two-factor authentication on your account.

On clicking the Enable button, you will get a notification on the dashboard informing you that the two-factor authentication security measure has been enabled.

As an additional security measure, you will also get an email from the Reloadly team informing you of this change.

Using two-factor authentication on your Reloadly account

After enabling the 2FA feature, you will be redirected to log in. When using the 2FA feature for the first time, there will be an option on the screen to scan a QR code using one of the authentication applications mentioned earlier

The following apps can be used to set up an authentication process and scan the QR code:

  • Google Authenticator
  • Auth0 Guardian
  • DUO Mobile
  • Authy

Disabling 2FA on your Reloadly account

To disable this feature, navigate to the dashboard and disable the feature in the Security section:
